## Methodologies Employed

### General Process Employed to Build BNs

Authors utilized an adapted version of the Knowledge Engineering of Bayesian Networks (KEBN) framework, initially introduced by Woodberry et al. ([25]). The modified procedure consists of three phases: 
1. **Structural Development**: Identify the critical components influencing effort and inter-variable connections. Factors incorporated include application attributes, stakeholder engagement, organizational culture, technical specifications, personnel skills, hardware, infrastructure, geographical location, regulatory issues, and economic conditions. Continuous variables often get converted into multinomial variables to meet the requirement for binary format input processing in the software toolset. Authors reviewed and refined the structure based on both theory-backed and empirical knowledge.

2. **Parameter Estimation**: Gather probabilities describing relationships among the entities discovered in the earlier phase. Probability calculations typically rely on data sources containing comprehensive statistics regarding multiple instances. Otherwise, manual expert judgment might replace probabilistic derivation from collected numerical data.

3. **Model Validation**: Evaluate the completeness, consistency, and correctness of the generated models. Initially, users test hypothetical business scenarios against the output produced by the model, ensuring it aligns closely with professional intuition. Next, utilizing historical records for verification checks allows determination of deviations in the forecasts generated versus actual performance indicators captured after completion.
